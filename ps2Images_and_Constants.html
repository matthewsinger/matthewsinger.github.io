Images and Constants

Programming Language BSL

Purpose Convert English into code, convert code into English, get practice with the docs and image library, and use constants to write clean code.

Setup At the top of your file, be sure to add (require 2htdp/image). Double check that DrRacket says Beginning Student Language in the lower left hand corner.

Exercises

Exercise 1 For each of the following constant names and English phrases, define a constant as that phrase converted into code. For example, for the constant name NINE and the English phrase four plus five, you would enter (define NINE (+ 4 5)) into your program.

Do not evaluate the expression (e.g., don’t write (define NINE 9)) or any of the sub-expressions. This is an exercise in translating literal English into code, which is an invaluable skill, because humans think in natural languages. As programmers, it’s our job to convert those thoughts into code.

  • CAT-DOG - the word cat combined with the word dog

  • BIG-CIRCLE - a solid, red circle with the radius of twenty five squared

  • MY-NAME-SIZE - the length of your name

  • MID-SUMMER - the two letters in the middle of the word summer

Exercise 2 As programmers, we must also be able to convert code into English, whether we’re reading our own code or someone else’s!

For each of the following expressions, place them in a single-line comment, and translate them into English. For example, given the expressions (+ 4 5), you should leave the following in your code:
; (+ 4 5)
; four plus five

If a function is unfamiliar to you, you can always experiment with it in the interactions window and/or look it up in the docs (Help > Racket Documentation). Remember to always click on the function in the correct language!

  • (above (circle 10 "solid" "blue") (square 20 "outline" "green"))

  • (replicate "echo" 5)

  • (+ 8 (sqrt 16) (string-length "argy-bargy"))

Exercise 3 Using the docs for aid, define a constant with a sensible name for each of the following:

  • A green triangle in a blue frame

  • A circle scaled only in the x axis above a green triangle in a blue frame

  • A chartreuse radial star placed somewhere in the upper-left quadrant of a brown rectangle

Exercise 4 Create a complex image of your choosing. You can make a house, a refrigerator, a cat, etc. Feel free to be as creative as you like, and rest assured you won’t be graded on aesthetic merit.

Your image should consist of multiple components and use and re-use constants where appropriate. Be sure to use proper indentation, too. Your final image should be defined as MY-IMAGE.

Below your image, leave a block comment describing the process you went through in creating it, and be sure to especially note any unexpected difficulties and how you handled them.
