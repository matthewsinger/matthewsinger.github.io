Problem Solving

Programming Language BSL

Purpose Break problems down into subproblems by utilizing helper functions, synthesize word problems into code

Setup Remember: think first, code later. One task per function!

Exercises

Exercise 1 Design a function which outputs a love letter (or, if you’re inclined, a breakup letter). The function should take in the components needed to generate letters for different people (so it might take in names, pronouns, titles, etc.), and it should be easily personalizable (so it might take in compliments, insults, dreams, fears, etc.). Your function should output a string, and be sure that each part of the letter is contained within its own helper. Your main function should be crisp, clear, and short, but generate a sizable letter.

Exercise 2 An aquarium is trying to figure out the dimensions for its new tank. The tank must be 2 meters tall, but they aren’t sure how deep or wide to make it.

Per regulations, the depth and width should add up to 2 meters.

For every cubic meter of the tank, they can add 4 fish.

For every fish, the aquarium is set to attract 2 patrons per day, each of which pay 10 dollars to enter the aquarium.

For every cubic meter of the tank, the aquarium will have to pay 50 cents to filter the water every day.

As the tank glass will need manual cleaning, for every square meter of glass, the aquarium has to pay 35 dollars. The tank is cleaned once a week, and has glass on all six sides.

There is an additional cleaning that occurs every day just for the side of the tank which faces patrons. As this is not as good of a cleaning, it only costs 30 dollars per square meter. Note that this cleaning happens even on days when the tank receives the full cleaning.

Help the aquarium by designing a function that will determine how much profit the tank makes per day given a width in meters.

Rounding to the nearest tenth (i.e. your answer should look something like .2 or 1.8), use your function to determine what the optimal width would be in meters.

Hint: you’re on the right track if your answer is between .5 and 1.5. Also, it will be helpful to convert weekly costs into daily costs, and cents into dollars.
